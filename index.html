The First Spark: AI's Genesis

1950: The Turing Test

Alan Turing proposes the "Imitation Game," a foundational concept for AI. It asks whether a machine can exhibit intelligent behavior equivalent to, or indistinguishable from, that of a human.

1956: Birth of "AI"

The Dartmouth Summer Research Project is considered the official birth of Artificial Intelligence as a field. John McCarthy coined the term and defined the core ambition.

1966: The First Chatbot

Joseph Weizenbaum creates ELIZA, an early natural language processing program that mimicked a non-directive psychotherapist. It showed how easily humans could attribute intelligence to machines.

The AI Advantage: Benefits Today

Revolutionizing Healthcare

AI accelerates drug discovery, improves diagnostic accuracy, and enables personalized treatment plans, saving countless lives.

Unmatched Efficiency

Automating repetitive tasks, AI reduces human error and allows teams to focus on creative, complex problem-solving and innovation.

Hyper-Personalization

From streaming recommendations to customized news feeds, AI tailors digital experiences to individual user preferences dynamically.

Accelerated Discovery

AI processes massive datasets to find patterns in climate science, physics, and genomics far faster than human analysis alone.
